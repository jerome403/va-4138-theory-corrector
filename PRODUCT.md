# Product

## Register

product

## Users

VA-accredited representatives, agents, and attorneys (and their support staff) at
Spearman Appeals LLC. The primary user is a practitioner doing case preparation at
a desk, frequently working from a veteran's existing profile/file. Their job: produce
a filing-ready VA Form 21-4138 ("Statement in Support of Claim") that corrects a
specific, recurring problem — the mismatch between the theory of entitlement the
veteran actually raised and the narrower theory implied by the AOJ's C&P exam
question. The ultimate beneficiary is the veteran whose claim depends on the
examiner answering the right legal question.

## Product Purpose

Generate a precise, citation-backed 21-4138 statement that (1) clarifies the claimed
theory of entitlement, (2) documents the mismatch with the AOJ's exam request, (3)
marshals the supporting evidence, and (4) requests corrected medical-opinion
questions. It exists to make legally rigorous filings fast and repeatable across many
cases. Success looks like: a print-ready, accurate statement produced with minimal
manual entry, with veteran PII handled entirely on-device and never transmitted.

## Brand Personality

Warm and advocacy-driven. Veteran-centered, human, and mission-forward — the file is
a person, and the tone reflects that. Voice is supportive, plain-spoken, and clear,
while still carrying the credibility the subject matter demands. Three words:
**advocacy, clarity, trust.** It should never read as cold federal bureaucracy.

## Anti-references

- Generic government-form drabness: gray-on-gray, cramped, intimidating, a faceless
  federal PDF.
- Soulless SaaS dashboard clichés: hero-metric cards, identical icon+heading+text
  card grids, "analytics" chrome that adds no meaning here.
- AI-slop landing aesthetics: cream/sand/parchment backgrounds, gradient text,
  tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, numbered section markers.
- Anything that makes a stressed veteran's case feel like a transaction.

## Design Principles

1. **Veteran dignity first.** The human behind the file is the point; clarity and
   tone are a form of advocacy, not decoration.
2. **Privacy is visible and absolute.** All PII stays on the device. The interface
   should make that commitment legible and trustworthy, never an afterthought.
3. **Legal precision without intimidation.** Exact citations and rigorous structure,
   delivered in plain, approachable language a practitioner can trust at a glance.
4. **Speed for the practitioner.** Repetitive case work demands low friction —
   auto-fill from existing profiles, live preview, and sane defaults over manual
   re-entry.
5. **Filing fidelity.** The generated document is the product. What the user sees in
   the preview is exactly what gets filed; output correctness outranks UI flourish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG conformance target is committed at this time. Baseline expectations:
legible contrast for body text, full keyboard operability of the form, and respect
for `prefers-reduced-motion`. Revisit and formalize (e.g. WCAG 2.1 AA) if veterans
or external users ever operate the tool directly rather than the practitioner.
